Our Story

The journey
that made
this space.

Sohamspaces was not planned. It emerged from a personal journey across four countries, a search for stillness that became a collection of teachings too important to keep alone.

Portugal
01

Portugal.
Where it began.

Growing up on the Silver Coast of Portugal, yoga was simply part of life. My mother ran her own yoga studio, and the practice was woven into daily life from the very beginning, steady and unhurried, like the Atlantic itself. Then came the decision to leave, to move to the UK for university, and with that distance came the first real question: what do I actually carry with me when everything familiar is left behind?

India
02

India.
Where it deepened.

Goa, home to the oldest yoga institute in the world, founded in 1918. Teachers whose words reshaped what yoga meant, not a physical practice, but a way of seeing. The Sanskrit names for states that had no English translation. The understanding that the practice was never separate from life. The first cards were written in notebooks here.

Bali
03

Bali.
Where it opened.

A month of volunteering, teaching yoga on a farm and living inside the real culture. The ceremonies witnessed at dawn, the offerings, the rituals woven into every ordinary moment, the understanding that devotion is not a separate practice but the quality of attention brought to whatever is in front of you. Bali gave the teachings of presence and of beauty as something practiced, not observed.

Malaysia
04

Malaysia.
Where it settled.

Teaching yoga in a hostel, for travellers passing through, people from every background and every level of experience. It was here that the practice became truly portable, stripped of any setting or expectation, just the teaching and whoever showed up. The realisation settled: this was never about a particular place. It had always been about what you carry inside it. The work of distilling it all into 83 cards began here.

The Founder

Not a teacher.
A fellow traveller.

I didn't create Sohamspaces to teach yoga. I created it because the teachings I encountered across four countries changed how I moved through the world, and I couldn't find a way to carry them with me that felt true. The cards are the answer I made for myself, and decided to share.

The cards are not a curriculum. They are a companion. Each one holds something I needed at a particular moment, and hope you might need too.

If you have questions, or want to share what a card meant to you, I'd love to hear from you.

What We Stand For

Four words.
One practice.

Satya

Truth

Everything here is rooted in lived experience, not theory. No teaching is shared that hasn't been tested.

Ahiṃsā

Non-harm

The practice is an invitation, never a demand. You bring what you have. That is always enough.

Santosha

Contentment

This is not a self-improvement project. It is a returning, to what you already are, beneath the noise.

Svādhyāya

Self-Study

Every card is a mirror. The practice of looking honestly, without judgment, is the whole of it.
